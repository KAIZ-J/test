Aklilu Lemma

Young Ethiopian doctor

Aklilu Lemma አክሊሉ ለማ





  • 1935 (September 18): Aklilu Lemma was born in Jijiga, Ethiopia.
  • Early Career: He studied biology at the University College in Addis Ababa and continued his education in the United States, earning a zoology degree from the University of Wisconsin, Madison.
  • 1964: Discovered endod's molluscicidal properties while at Johns Hopkins University, where he also obtained his doctorate.
  • 1966: Established the Institute of Pathobiology at Haile Selassie I University (now Addis Ababa University) in Addis Ababa.
  • 1966-1970: Served as Dean of the Faculty of Science and Director of the Institute of Pathobiology.
  • 1969: Founded the International Centre for Insect Physiology and Endocrinology in Nairobi, Kenya.
  • 1970-1972: Undertook sabbatical leave in the US, conducting research at Stanford Research Institute.
  • 1989: Awarded the Right Livelihood Award.
  • 1993-1997: While at Johns Hopkins University, he focused on building scientific capacity in Africa, particularly in HIV/AIDS control in Ethiopia and Uganda.
  • 1997 (April 5): Died at the age of 62.

" We have learned the hard way that the root problems of scientific research in Africa are not only the lack of facilities and funds but also the biases of individuals and organisations in industrialised countries. "

~~Aklilu Lemma, 1989 Laureate
